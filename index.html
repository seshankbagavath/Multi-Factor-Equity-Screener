The Model

Five factor families, one score

Each family is z-scored cross-sectionally, averaged, then combined with renormalized weights so partial coverage never penalizes a name.

Value

Earnings, book, and EBITDA yields — inverted so cheaper is better. Winsorized at the point of inversion.

25% weight

Profitability

Return on equity & assets, gross and net margins — quality metrics, already oriented higher-is-better.

25% weight

Momentum

12-1 price momentum from the price panel, skipping the most recent month. Full-history filter kills spinoff artifacts.

20% weight

Volatility

Annualized realized volatility, inverted for a low-vol tilt — calmer names score higher.

15% weight

Growth

Revenue and earnings growth — capturing the trajectory of the underlying business.

15% weight

Composite

Weighted sum of family z-scores, renormalized per stock over available data, ranked into deciles 1–10.

final score
Pipeline

How it works

From raw market data to a ranked leaderboard in four stages — every network call is fault-tolerant and cached.

1

Collect

Batch-pull 2y prices and per-ticker fundamentals via yfinance, cached to disk for 24h.

2

Clean

Coerce types, neutralize infinities, winsorize outliers, and impute with the sector median.

3

Score

Cross-sectional z-score, average within families, weight, and combine into one composite.

4

Rank & visualize

Bucket into deciles and export a leaderboard CSV plus a 4-panel interactive dashboard.

🛡 Data-quality engineering

Real market data is messy. The guiding rule throughout: winsorize every derived metric where it is created, not just the raw inputs.

  • Spinoff momentum artifact — a recently spun-off ticker's discontinuous price history produced an 18-sigma outlier. Fixed with a minimum-history filter and price-factor winsorization.
  • Corrupted valuation ratio — a near-zero price-to-book inverted into an absurd book yield (~576). Fixed by winsorizing inverted metrics at the point of creation.
